Motion Design & Product Storytelling · 2024–25

Arnifi Motion

Weekly impressions more than doubled, 15,073 to 32,193, on the back of 15+ product films. Every Arnifi feature launch got a story, not a screenshot; I wrote, designed, and animated each film end-to-end in After Effects.
Role, Motion + Product Designer
Team, Arnifi, Dubai
Scope, 15+ films, launch to social
Tools, After Effects · Figma
01 / The brief
Company formation is paperwork. The films had to make it feel like momentum.
Arnifi helps founders set up businesses in the UAE and beyond, a product that lives in forms, documents, and government processes. Static screenshots made every launch look like admin software. The bet: treat each feature release as a 30–60 second story with real pacing, typography, and rhythm, motion that respects a B2B audience's time while giving the brand a pulse.

Two features, one motion language.
The explainer films share a system, same easing curves, same type hierarchy, same device framing, so every launch compounds brand recognition instead of starting from zero. Motion tokens, effectively.

04 / The hardest brief
Arnifi Docs
Document management, the least glamorous feature in the product, and the one users touch most. The film gives paperwork a sense of order arriving: documents snap into place, statuses resolve, chaos becomes a system.
Feature launch · LinkedIn + web
05 / What this taught me
Motion is a system, the same as any component library.
Fifteen films only stayed consistent because the easing, pacing, and type behavior were designed once and reused, the same discipline as the design system I was building for the product at the same time. That's the throughline of my work: whether it's components or keyframes, the craft is in the grammar, not the one-off.
